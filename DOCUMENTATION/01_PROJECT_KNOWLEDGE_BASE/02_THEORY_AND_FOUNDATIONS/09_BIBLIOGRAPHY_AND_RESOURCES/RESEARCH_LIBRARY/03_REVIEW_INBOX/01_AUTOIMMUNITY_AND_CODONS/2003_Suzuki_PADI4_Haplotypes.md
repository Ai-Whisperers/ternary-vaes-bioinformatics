# Functional haplotypes of PADI4, encoding citrullinating enzyme peptidylarginine deiminase 4, are associated with rheumatoid arthritis

**Author:** Suzuki, A., et al. (RIKEN)
**Year:** 2003
**Journal:** Nature Genetics
**Link:** [PubMed Record](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12845329/)
**Tags:** #padi4 #ra #citrullination #genetics #seminal

## Abstract

The seminal paper establishing _PADI4_ as the first non-HLA genetic risk factor for Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA). It discovered that specific haplotypes of the PADI4 gene increase the stability of the mRNA, leading to excess production of the enzyme PAD4. This enzyme performs **Citrullination** (Arg -> Cit), generating the "neo-antigens" that the immune system attacks (ACPA antibodies).

## Key Genetic Data

### 1. The Risk Haplotype

- **Haplotype:** `GTG` (Susceptible) vs `non-GTG` (Protective).
- **Odds Ratio (OR):**
  - Original Japanese Cohort: Highly significant association.
  - Western Mexican Cohort (Replication): **OR = 2.86** ($p = 0.024$).
  - Global Meta-analysis: **OR ~ 1.14 - 1.46**.

### 2. Functional Mechanism

- **mRNA Stability:** The risk haplotype increases the half-life of PADI4 mRNA.
- **Enzymatic Overload:** More mRNA $\to$ More PAD4 Enzyme $\to$ **Hyper-Citrullination** of proteins (Histones, Fibrinogen, etc.).
- **Autoimmunity:** The immune system tolerates Arginine but attacks Citrulline (which mimics Glutamine in p-adic space?).

## Relevance to Project

**The "P-adic Mutation."**

- Citrullination is effectively a "Post-Translational Mutation" of Arg (+ Charge) to Cit (Neutral).
- **P-adic Analysis:** Does Citrulline look "closer" to a viral antigen in p-adic space than Arginine does?
